## WEEK 1: JANUARY 6-12, 2026 (THIS WEEK)
### Legal & Business Structure ‚ö° CRITICAL

#### Business Formation
- [ ] **File DBA** for EV Expansion Group under existing LLC
  - Timeline: 1-2 business days online
  - Cost: ~$25-50 filing fee
  - Link: [Texas SOS DBA Filing](https://www.sos.state.tx.us/)
  
- [ ] **Update EIN documentation** to include DBA
  - Contact IRS or use existing EIN as-is (DBA typically doesn't require new EIN)
  
- [ ] **Register business with local county** (Dallas/Tarrant/Collin county clerk)
  - Required for DBA - varies by county
  - Cost: ~$20-100
  - Timeline: Same day to 3 days

#### Banking & Financial
- [ ] **Open business bank account** under DBA name
  - Bring: LLC docs, DBA certificate, EIN letter, ID
  - Recommended: Chase, Bank of America (good for business credit)
  
- [ ] **Apply for business credit card** (if not already done)
  - Use for expenses, build credit
  - Look for 0% intro APR cards for cash flow

- [ ] **Set up QuickBooks or accounting software**
  - Essential for tracking government contracts
  - Timeline: 1 day setup

#### Insurance (START NOW - Can take 7-14 days)
- [ ] **General Liability Insurance** ($1-3M coverage)
  - Cost: $500-2,000/year
  - Contact: Progressive, Hiscox, or local broker
  
- [ ] **Professional Liability/E&O Insurance**
  - Covers installation errors
  - Cost: $500-1,500/year
  
- [ ] **Commercial Auto Insurance** (if using vehicle for business)
  
- [ ] **Workers Compensation** (if hiring employees - not for 1099 contractors)
  - Required in Texas if you have employees

## ONGOING: GOVERNMENT PROGRAMS & GRANTS

### Federal Programs
#### 1. **NEVI Formula Program (National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure)**
- **What:** $5 billion for EV charging infrastructure
- **Focus:** Highways, public charging corridors
- **Action:** Contact Texas Department of Transportation (TxDOT)
  - They administer NEVI funds in Texas
  - Link: [TxDOT NEVI Program](https://www.txdot.gov/)
  - Timeline: Apply when RFPs are released (quarterly/annually)

#### 2. **CFI (Charging and Fueling Infrastructure) Grants**
- **What:** $2.5 billion for community charging
- **Who:** Cities, counties, tribes, nonprofits (you can be installer partner)
- **Action:** Partner with municipalities for applications
- **Timeline:** Rolling applications

#### 3. **Federal Tax Credits (30C Commercial Clean Vehicle Credit)**
- **What:** 30% tax credit up to $100K per charger
- **Who qualifies:** Business owners installing chargers
- **Action:** Help customers claim this in their taxes
- **Marketing angle:** "We help you save 30% on installation"

#### 4. **USDA Rural Energy for America Program (REAP)**
- **What:** Grants + loans for rural businesses
- **Action:** Target rural Texas businesses/farms

### Texas State Programs
#### 1. **Texas Emissions Reduction Plan (TERP)**
- **What:** Grants for alt-fuel infrastructure
- **Who:** Texas businesses, especially fleets
- **Action:** Check Texas Commission on Environmental Quality (TCEQ)
- **Timeline:** Annual funding rounds

#### 2. **Texas VW Environmental Mitigation Trust**
- **What:** Settlement funds for EV infrastructure
- **Action:** Check TCEQ website for open programs

### Utility Company Programs
#### 1. **Oncor (Dallas/Fort Worth)**
- EV charging rebates for commercial customers
- Contact their business solutions department

#### 2. **Texas Electric Cooperatives**
- Many offer EV charger incentives
- Check specific co-ops in your service areas

## ONGOING: GOVERNMENT CONTRACT STRATEGY

### Federal Contracts
- [ ] **Search for opportunities:**
  - [Beta.SAM.gov](https://beta.sam.gov/) - search "EV charging" "EVSE"
  - Set up saved searches with email alerts
  
- [ ] **Target agencies:**
  - General Services Administration (GSA)
  - Department of Defense (military bases)
  - US Postal Service (they're electrifying fleet)
  - Veterans Affairs (hospitals)
  - National Park Service

- [ ] **Get on GSA Schedule** (after 6-12 months of operation)
  - Makes it easy for feds to buy from you
  - Timeline: 6-9 months to get approved
  - Cost: ~$5K-15K (can DIY or hire consultant)

### State & Local Government
- [ ] **Texas DIR (Department of Information Resources) Cooperative Contracts**
  - Once on a DIR contract, all TX state agencies can buy from you
  - Link: [Texas DIR](https://dir.texas.gov/)
  
- [ ] **Contact directly:**
  - [ ] Texas A&M University System
  - [ ] University of Texas System
  - [ ] School districts (ISD fleet programs)
  - [ ] City facilities departments
  - [ ] County facilities
  
- [ ] **Join Texas Association of School Boards (TASB) vendor list**
  - School districts are buying chargers for buses

### Strategy Tips:
1. **Start small:** Subcontract on larger projects before bidding prime contracts
2. **Team up:** Partner with established government contractors
3. **Past performance is king:** Document every successful install meticulously
4. **Respond quickly:** Government buyers value responsiveness
5. **Build relationships:** Go to pre-bid meetings, introduce yourself
