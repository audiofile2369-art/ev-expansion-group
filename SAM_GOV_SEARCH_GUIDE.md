## ðŸš¨ PRO TIPS FOR SAM.GOV

### 1. Set Up Saved Searches
- Click "Save Search" after filtering
- Get email alerts for new opportunities
- Check daily or weekly

### 2. Create Multiple Saved Searches
- Save 5-10 different keyword combinations
- You'll catch opportunities others miss

### 3. Read "Sources Sought" Notices
- These are pre-solicitation research requests
- Respond even if you're not bidding yet
- Gets your name in front of contracting officers
- Shows government you exist

### 4. Look for "Sources Sought" + "Small Business"
- These are perfect for new companies
- Government wants to know who can do the work
- Low risk way to introduce yourself

### 5. Check "Opportunities Expiring Soon"
- Filter by closing date
- Sometimes great opportunities have few bidders

### 6. Read Past Awards
- Go to "Contract Data" section
- Search same keywords
- See what was awarded, to whom, for how much
- Learn what government actually buys

## â“ COMMON SAM.GOV QUESTIONS

**Q: Do I need to be registered to search?**
A: No, anyone can search. But you MUST register to bid.

**Q: How long does registration take?**
A: 2-4 weeks for full approval. Start NOW.

**Q: What if I have no past performance?**
A: Start with "Sources Sought" responses, small set-asides, and subcontracting to build references.

**Q: Should I bid on every opportunity?**
A: NO. Only bid on opportunities you can actually win and perform. Quality > quantity.

**Q: What's a capability statement?**
A: One-page PDF about your company. Include: services, certifications, contact info, UEI number.
