## üö® COMMON OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES

### "Sounds too good to be true"
**Response:** "I understand - it does sound amazing! But this is federal policy to accelerate EV infrastructure. The government allocated $5 billion through the Infrastructure Act. The money is real and available now. Want to see the official TxDOT website?"

### "I don't have time for government paperwork"
**Response:** "That's exactly why we exist. We complete 100% of the paperwork. Your only job is to sign the application. We handle everything else - forms, documentation, follow-up, everything."

### "What if my grant is denied?"
**Response:** "We have a high approval rate because we pre-qualify properties and know exactly what grant administrators want. But if it happens, we help you reapply or find alternative funding. With our success fee option, you only pay if the grant is approved - zero risk."

### "I don't want to offer public charging"
**Response:** "That's required for NEVI-funded chargers. But think of it as a revenue stream - you set the pricing. Many property owners charge $0.35-0.50 per kWh and generate $500-2,000 per month per charger. It pays for itself quickly."

### "How much maintenance is required?"
**Response:** "EV chargers are like ATMs - minimal maintenance. SK Signet chargers have a warranty, and we offer maintenance packages. Most owners spend less than $100/month on maintenance per charger."

### "Can I install just 1 charger?"
**Response:** "NEVI requires minimum 4 DC fast chargers per site. But for Level 2 chargers using tax credits only, you can start with 1-2. We can design a phased approach - start small, expand later."

## ‚úÖ YOUR PROCESS CHECKLIST

### When a Client Says "Yes":

#### Step 1: Site Assessment (Week 1)
- [ ] Schedule site visit
- [ ] Bring camera, measuring tape, notepad
- [ ] Photograph electrical panel
- [ ] Photograph proposed charger locations
- [ ] Measure distances
- [ ] Note electrical service size (look at main breaker)
- [ ] Take property owner contact info

#### Step 2: Quote & Grant Prep (Week 1-2)
- [ ] Calculate electrical load
- [ ] Price equipment from SK Signet
- [ ] Calculate installation costs (get quote from electrician)
- [ ] Identify applicable grants
- [ ] Calculate total funding available
- [ ] Create professional quote
- [ ] Include timeline and terms

#### Step 3: Contract Signing (Week 2)
- [ ] Client signs installation contract
- [ ] Collect deposit (typically 25-50%)
- [ ] Client authorizes grant application

#### Step 4: Grant Submission (Week 2-3)
- [ ] Complete grant application forms
- [ ] Attach site photos and documentation
- [ ] Include technical specifications
- [ ] Client signs application
- [ ] Submit to appropriate agency (TxDOT, etc.)
- [ ] Get confirmation receipt

#### Step 5: Approval & Ordering (Week 4-8)
- [ ] Track grant status
- [ ] Respond to any questions from grant administrators
- [ ] Once approved, order equipment from SK Signet
- [ ] Schedule electrical contractor

#### Step 6: Permitting (Week 8-10)
- [ ] Submit permit application to city
- [ ] Include electrical plans
- [ ] Include equipment specs
- [ ] Include contractor license
- [ ] Pay permit fees
- [ ] Wait for approval (1-7 days expedited, or 2-4 weeks)

#### Step 7: Installation (Week 10-12)
- [ ] Equipment delivered to site
- [ ] Electrician performs installation
- [ ] You supervise (be on-site for first few installs)
- [ ] Test chargers
- [ ] Schedule city final inspection

#### Step 8: Final Inspection & Go-Live (Week 12)
- [ ] City inspector signs off
- [ ] Activate chargers on network
- [ ] Train property owner/staff
- [ ] Provide user manuals
- [ ] Set pricing (if applicable)
- [ ] Collect final payment

#### Step 9: Documentation & Marketing (Week 12+)
- [ ] Take photos of completed install
- [ ] Get customer testimonial
- [ ] Request Google review
- [ ] Create case study
- [ ] Submit completion docs to grant administrator
- [ ] Help client claim tax credits (provide IRS documentation)
